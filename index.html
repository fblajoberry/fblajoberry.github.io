Interviews

Interview Information: The purpose of a Job Interview: To convey to the employer that you want you’re the perfect candidate for the job. To become more knowledgeable about the job and company To explore your interests regarding the job and employer For the employer to assess if you are a good fit for the position. Telephone Interviews (pre-screening): Some employers will call the individual before bringing them into the company for an interview; this is known as a telephone interview. They will determine if you are qualified for the position during the call. The purpose of this call is to pre-screen the candidate. You should answer their questions as if you are having a face to face interview, as this call will determine if you are qualified or not. Here are some suggestions to follow. Keep a copy of your resume nearby and a copy of the job description Make sure you have a paper and pen nearby to take notes You need to be focused and organized, know when is a good and bad time for the employer to call you. While you are on the phone, sit straight and smile. You will come off as positive and clear. Try to keep your answers concise and to the point. Support all the points you put on your resume and talk about past achievements You should have already researched the company. Make sure you indicate to your employer that you have taken the time to research about their company as this will express your interest. What to do before the interview: Research the company and be informed of it’s products and services. The employer will be impressed by your knowledge. Make sure you understand which questions are inappropriate for an employer to ask you during an interview. You must also be prepared how to deal with the situation if you are asked the question. Get a copy of the job description so you are familiar with the responsibilities. Have you references neatly typed on a seperate paper. Make sure you inform the references about the upcoming interview. Practise possible questions and answers. This will make you less nervous during the interview. Think about the impression you want to create; your work experiences, skills, personal characteristics and achievements should be tailored to the employer’s needs. Review your experiences from previous interviews. If you made mistakes in the past do not repeat them Prepare the questions you want to ask the employer. Make sure they are relevant and convey your interest in the position. Prepare what you want to wear the night before Leave early to be on time for the interview. Confirm the date, time, and location before the day of the interview. What to do during the interview: Greet the interview with a smile. Shake his/her hand firmly and thank them for their time. Accompany the interviewer to their office, wait until asked to be seated. Place your jacket and bag in the appropriate location Do not state false information about past experiences and skills Allow the interviewer to ask the questions, refrain from using a passive tone. Always remember to be positive and optimistic throughout the entire interview. If you have an accent, be aware of it. Make sure you are speaking clear and the interviewer can understand you.
